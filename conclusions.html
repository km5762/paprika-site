Conclusions

There are a few different conclusions that we are able to draw in relation to computing and society through the lens of the Paprika movie. The first has to do with the regulation of technology. Lots of new and innovative technology such as AI in the real world and the DCMini in Paprika have the potential to do great things for society but also cause great harm for society. 37% of people said they are more concerned about the changes that come with AI than the potential benefits [1], and in the movie, many of the harms of the DCMini (lack of authentication, making people go mentally crazy) were highlighted more than its potential upsides (psychotherapy). Over 100x the people are working in AI in comparison to working on its regulations to keep it safe [2]. Because of this we have come to the conclusion that more steps need to be taken to regulate new and upcoming technologies like these. One way this can be done is expanding general knowledge about the technology and its capabilities. In the movie. Only a select few individuals knew how to use the DCMini and all its capabilities. If that information was spread out more, it could have prevented some of the tragedies that happened to people like Doctor Torataro Shima when he jumped out the window in his crazed mental state.

Another conclusion that we drew had to deal with the accountability for misuse of technology in a virtual world. Throughout the movie many malicious things were done inside of the dreamscape that didn’t and did end up having effects on the real world around it. To what level should these people be accountable for what they do in the DCMini’s virtual space, and to another extent, our online space? The internet allows for an unparalleled amount of anonymity as seen by Dr. Chiba Atsuko took on a whole other persona (Paprika) when she entered the DCMini’s dreamscape. (CONCLUSION)
